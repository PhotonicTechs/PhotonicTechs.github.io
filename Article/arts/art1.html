There are so many planets we have discovered in our galaxy which lie in the habitable zone of
their host star, but barely any of the discovered planets are Earth-like. If the planet is extremely large, it’s
most likely a gas planet, if too small, the low gravity won’t be strong enough to hold an atmosphere, a
planet without an atmosphere isn’t the most ideal environment for life. This is why searching for planets
having a similar radius to earth’s in the habitable zone is much more likely to be habitable for life than
looking for any planet in all sorts of sizes in the habitable zone. These following planets you see here
may be habitable, but what makes Kepler 186f stand out is it being the first planet to be discovered
having a similar radius to Earths in the habitable zone. This amazing discovery proved that there can be

other earth-sized planets in the habitable zone, not just earth. Kepler 186f resides in the Kepler 186
system 557.7 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Cygnus. Kepler 186f coexists among 4 other
planets in its system. They are pretty close to their star to where they make a complete orbit in less than
23 days but Kepler 186f orbits its star in 130 days because it’s further out in the habitable zone. Kepler
186f is the second smallest planet in its system with a radius of 4,631.7 miles, estimated to be around 27
to 33 percent bigger than Earth's. It’s also believed to be at least 2 times the size of the earth. Now, just

because this planet is in the habitable zone of its star, doesn’t mean it’s habitable. Since the planet is
about 582 light-years away, we don’t know what it’s made up of. We aren’t even sure about its
atmosphere and temperature. As you may know, the temperature of a planet is highly affected by just its
atmosphere. That’s why Venus is hotter than Mercury even though Mercury is closer to our sun; Venus has
a thick atmosphere primarily made up of carbon dioxide, most radiation from our sun has a wavelength
small enough to pass through the atmosphere, once the surface of the planet absorbs the energy that
has passed through, it releases it up towards space in a form of thermal radiation with longer
wavelengths than before. The atmosphere is now capable of absorbing some heat from the radiation

since its larger wavelength makes it less opaque to the atmosphere. With Venus extremely thick
atmosphere, no wonder why it’s so hot. Mercury, on the other hand, has a thin exosphere mostly made up
of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium atoms blasted off the surface from solar winds and
striking meteoroids which will hold in barely any heat from the sun. Now, imagine if Kepler 186f having
an atmosphere like Venus. It won’t actually be scorching hot because it’s a lot further away from its star
than Venus is from our star and Kepler 186 is an M-dwarf star with a radius size 48 percent smaller than
our star and a surface temperature at around 2,023 Kelvins less than our star. If the planet has a rather
thick atmosphere with high levels of carbon dioxide like Venus, it may be enough to warm the planet to
where water can flow on its surface with temperatures just above 0 degrees Celsius for life to evolve
under the right conditions. How about Mercury? Well, the planet’s temperature would be around its

equilibrium temperature which is the surface temperature of a planet without an atmosphere. It is
estimated to be around -85 degrees Celsius which is below freezing. With the planet in this state, it will
not be able to support life. As you can tell, a planet’s atmosphere is extremely important in determining
habitability. Now a planet’s axial tilt may seem irrelevant in this equation but it also plays a big part in
finding a habitable planet. Earth has seasons because of our planets axis rotation is tilted at a pretty
consistent angle of 23.5 degrees year-round. This allows for different parts of our planet to vary in
temperature through its full rotation cycle around our star giving our planet seasons. If a planet does not
have an axis tilt, the planet's equator will always have the most amount of light while other parts of the
planet will have the least amount of light. The temperature change in the regions of the planet will be
very consistent if there aren’t any other season-changing influences like varied distance change from its

star as it rotates around. As you would expect, the closer you are to the star, the hotter the planet will be,
the further, the colder. If Kepler 186f had no axial tilt, the only major temperature fluctuation it will
experience is its distance change in orbiting its star. Fortunately, the probability of it being tidally locked
is lower because tidal effects are much weaker on planets in a higher orbit. Another big mystery is Kepler
186f’s compositions. Since the planet’s diameter is somewhat similar to Earth’s, scientists believe it may
also be a rocky planet since planets around this size are usually rocky in the habitable zone, but some
research well after the discovery of Kepler 186f estimates earth-like planets in the habitable zone are
possibly between 2 and 12 percent likely to be a rocky planet, but much more data is needed for a much
more accurate estimation. Even if it were a rocky planet, if it doesn’t have similar compositions as Earths,
this may or may not be a big problem depending on what’s missing. There are plenty of elements our
planet has that life doesn’t need to survive so a bit of uniqueness is fine. Since there are many unknown

things about this planet, the chances of all of these vital requirements being met feels unlikely to me
personally, so I doubt this planet will be habitable, but with a great thick atmosphere with plenty
enough carbon dioxide to hold in enough heat so that the temperature can be above 0 degrees Celsius, a
reasonable axial tilt, and being a rocky planet with essential compositions including water, this planet
may absolutely be habitable for life. Even though there are other planets claimed to be more earth-like
than Kepler 186f in the habitable zone, it will always be remembered as the first Earth-sized exoplanet
found in the goldilocks zone.
